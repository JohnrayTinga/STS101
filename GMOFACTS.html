GMO FACTS

WHAT IS GMO?

Under the Non-GMO Project Standard, a genetically modified organism (GMO) is an organism to which biotechnology has been applied. This creates combinations of plant, animal, bacteria and virus genes that do not occur in nature or through traditional crossbreeding methods.

WHICH FOODS MIGHT CONTAIN GMO'S?

Most packaged and processed foods contain ingredients from genetically modified corn, soy, canola, and sugar beet.The Non-GMO Project also flags animal-derived products like eggs, milk, meat, honey, and seafood as high risk due to the prevalence of genetically engineered ingredients in animal feed.

ARE GMO'S LABELED?

Sixty-five countries, including Australia, Japan, and all EU nations, require GMO labeling, but Canada does not. The USDA's National Bioengineered Food Disclosure Standard mandates some GMO labeling in the U.S., but exemptions limit full transparency.

WHAT ARE THE IMPAPCTS OF GMO'S ON THE ENVIRONMENT?

Most genetically modified crops are engineered for herbicide tolerance, leading to a fifteenfold increase in herbicide use over 20 years. This harms biodiversity and creates resistant “superweeds” and “superbugs,” necessitating more toxic chemicals. Developed by major chemical companies, GMOs are an extension of chemical agriculture with unknown long-term impacts and cannot be recalled once released.

HOW DO GMO'S AFFECT FARMER?

Biotechnology companies patent genetically engineered seeds, limiting their use and distribution. Restrictive licenses prevent farmers from saving seeds, making them dependent on corporations for seeds and herbicides. This reliance threatens farmer sovereignty and national food security.

ARE GMO'S SAFE??

In the absence of credible, independent, long-term feeding studies, the safety of GMOs is unknown.
